ABOUT ME

Hi, I'm Anna. I'm a Personal Development coach based in Central Hawkes Bay. I set up Complete Coaching Hawkes Bay after finishing my Diploma in Life Coaching from the New Zealand Institute of Business. We are living in uncertain times and I want to help!
Life is not always perfect. It is a series of phases and we sometimes need to re-set, check in on ourselves, on our goals and dreams and sometimes we just need some motivation and empowerment.
Our minds are very powerful and sometimes negative self talk can influence the way we think and can affect our day to day life. Coaching helps identify how we think and if needed, how we can change the way we think so we are in a better positive space to achieve success.
Coaching is a process that creates mental space. It's about closing the gap between dreams and reality.
If you are feeling disorganised, unfulfilled, lacking direction, lacking motivation, lacking confidence, needing encouragement or needing to set priorities then I can help you.


Our Values

I live in Takapau with my husband Tom and daughters, Harper and Darcie. Until recently I worked for the Department of Corrections before deciding to fulfill my dream of becoming a Personal Development/Life Coach. Within my 12 years employed for the Department of Corrections, I have served as a Probation Officer, Case Manager, Professional Supervisor and Practice Leader working with a diverse group of people. I am trained in motivational interviewing which is a huge part of Coaching and something I have been doing for years with clients and colleagues. I have a Diploma in Life Coaching and also recently completed some papers in Careers Counselling as I am passionate about helping people into careers that are well thought out and planned as we live in changing and uncertain times. I have also completed a Bachelor of Arts in Social Services (Otago University) and a Post Gradutate Diploma in Primary Teaching (Victoria University) so interest and drive has always been about people and making a positive difference.
